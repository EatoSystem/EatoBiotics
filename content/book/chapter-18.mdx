## Who Should Use the 21-Day Build

**Use the 21-Day Build if:**

- You're new to gut health optimisation and starting from a standard Western diet (low fiber, processed foods)
- You've been eating poorly for months or years and want a structured approach to rebuild
- You've taken multiple rounds of antibiotics recently and need to restore your microbiome
- You have digestive issues (IBS, chronic bloating, irregularity) and need a gentle, gradual approach
- You've tried to eat healthy before but it felt overwhelming — you need a step-by-step progression
- You did the 7-Day Reset and want to continue with a longer, deeper protocol
- You want to establish the EatoBiotics approach so solidly that it becomes your default

<ChapterCallout type="warning">
**Don't Use the 21-Day Build If:**

- You've already been following the EatoBiotics approach consistently — just continue
- You only need a quick recalibration — use the 7-Day Reset instead
- You have severe digestive conditions requiring medical supervision — work with a gastroenterologist and a registered dietitian (you can still use elements of this approach with professional guidance)
- You're recovering from an eating disorder — work with a practitioner who can adapt this approach to your specific needs
</ChapterCallout>

## The Three-Phase Structure

<ChapterStat value="21 days" label="three phases" sublabel="Foundation → Expansion → Optimisation" color="green" />

The 21-Day Build is divided into three phases, each lasting one week:

**Week 1: Foundation (Days 1–7)**

- Goal: Establish baseline habits
- Fiber target: 15–20g daily
- Fermented foods: 1–2 servings daily
- Focus: Getting comfortable with the basics

**Week 2: Expansion (Days 8–14)**

- Goal: Increase volume and variety
- Fiber target: 25–30g daily
- Fermented foods: 2–3 servings daily
- Focus: Diversifying plant intake, building tolerance

**Week 3: Optimisation (Days 15–21)**

- Goal: Reach full EatoBiotics targets
- Fiber target: 30–40g daily
- Fermented foods: 3 servings daily
- Focus: Making it automatic, troubleshooting remaining issues

Each phase builds on the previous one. Don't skip ahead — the gradual progression prevents digestive distress and builds sustainable habits.

**Goals:**

### Week 1 Challenges

**"I'm constantly hungry"**

- You're probably not eating enough volume — these aren't tiny portions
- Add more protein (eggs, yogurt, legumes)
- Add more healthy fats (avocado, nuts, olive oil)
- Eat more frequently if needed (3 meals + 2 snacks)

**"I'm experiencing strong cravings for sugar/processed foods"**

- This is normal Days 1–4, peaks around Day 3, usually significantly better by Day 7
- Drink water when cravings hit
- Go for a walk
- Have a piece of fruit if you need something sweet
- Remind yourself this is temporary

**"I'm getting headaches"**

- Likely caffeine or sugar withdrawal
- If you normally drink 4 cups of coffee, don't go to zero — reduce to 2 cups
- Drink plenty of water, get adequate sleep
- Should resolve by Day 4–5

**"I'm not pooping regularly"**

- Increase water dramatically (aim for 10–12 cups daily)
- Increase healthy fats (they lubricate the digestive tract)
- Move more (walking stimulates peristalsis)
- This often resolves by Week 2 as your system adapts

### Week 2 Challenges

**"I'm experiencing bloating and gas now that fiber is higher"**

- This is normal adaptation
- Slow down the fiber increase — stay at 22–25g instead of pushing to 30g
- Emphasise cooked vegetables over raw
- Chew thoroughly (20–30 times per bite)
- Walk after meals, drink fennel or peppermint tea
- This usually improves significantly by Week 3

**"I'm bored with the food"**

- Rotate your vegetables — try one new vegetable this week
- Change your spice profiles (Mediterranean one day, Asian the next, Mexican the next)
- Look for inspiration (food blogs, cookbooks, social media)
- Remember: variety within the template is infinite

**"I'm struggling to hit the fiber targets"**

- Make sure you're including legumes at lunch and dinner (they're fiber powerhouses)
- Add ground flaxseed or chia seeds to meals (easy fiber boost)
- Use frozen vegetables liberally (as easy as fresh, counts the same)
- Track for a day to see where you're falling short

### Week 3 Challenges

**"I feel great but I'm worried about maintaining this"**

- This is common — acknowledge the fear
- Remember: You've proven you can do this for 21 days
- Maintenance doesn't require perfection — 80/20 rule
- You have the 7-Day Reset as a tool if you drift
- Trust yourself

**"I hit a plateau in how I feel"**

- Plateaus are normal — you're likely still improving, just less dramatically
- Fine-tune based on what you've learned (maybe you need slightly less fiber, or more sleep, or different fermented foods)
- The goal is sustainable wellbeing, not constantly feeling "better and better"

**"I'm anxious about Day 22 and beyond"**

- The habits are established — now you maintain them with flexibility
- See the "After the Build" section below

## After the 21-Day Build: Transition to Sustainable Maintenance

### Week 4 and Beyond

You have two options:

**Option 1: Continue As-Is (Recommended for most people)**

- Keep following the EatoBiotics Plate at most meals
- Maintain 30–40g fiber daily
- Maintain 2–3 fermented food servings daily
- Allow flexibility when it makes sense (social occasions, travel, treats)
- Follow the 80/20 rule: 80% of meals follow the framework, 20% are flexible

This is sustainable indefinitely.

**Option 2: Ease Up Slightly, Then Observe**

- Reduce fiber to 25–30g if 30–40g felt like a lot
- Reduce fermented foods to 1–2 servings daily if 3 felt like too much
- See how you feel after 1–2 weeks
- If symptoms start returning, you know your personal threshold
- Adjust back up to the level where you felt best

<ChapterCallout type="science">
**The Personal Sweet Spot**

Most people feel best with 30–35g fiber daily (the sweet spot for most people), 2–3 fermented food servings daily, following the EatoBiotics Plate at 80–90% of meals, and allowing treats, dining out, and flexibility without guilt. Your personal sweet spot may be slightly higher or lower — the 21-Day Build helps you discover it.
</ChapterCallout>

## The Build Mindset

Here's the most important thing to understand:

**The 21-Day Build isn't about perfection. It's about progression.**

- If you miss a target one day, return to it the next day
- If Week 2 feels too aggressive, repeat Week 1 for another week before advancing
- If you have a day where you eat off-plan, just return to the Build at your next meal
- If you experience persistent digestive issues, slow down the progression

The goal is to end these 21 days with:

- Established habits
- Improved health
- Confidence in your ability to maintain this

That's success. Not perfection. Not rigid adherence. **Sustainable transformation.**

You're not building a temporary diet. You're building a lifetime system.
