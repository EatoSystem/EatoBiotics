You understand the science. You have the kitchen setup. You know what foods support your microbiome.

This is where theory meets practice. This is where you need a simple, visual formula that works every single time — whether you're cooking at home, eating at a restaurant, or assembling lunch at work.

The EatoBiotics Plate is that formula.

It's a signature template that ensures every meal you eat supports your microbiome, stabilises your blood sugar, provides adequate protein and healthy fats, and delivers the fiber and diversity your gut needs to thrive.

The formula is simple:

**2 Plants + Protein + Fat + Ferment + Booster**

That's it. Five components. Every meal.

## The EatoBiotics Plate: Visual Breakdown

Imagine your plate divided into sections:

**50% = 2 PLANTS (Fiber Foundation)**

- Plant 1: Vegetables (raw, cooked, or fermented)
- Plant 2: Legumes, whole grains, starchy vegetables, or more vegetables

**25% = PROTEIN (Quality Protein)**

- Animal or plant-based protein
- Legumes count as both Plant 2 and Protein (they're dual-purpose)

**Integrated = FAT (Healthy Fats)**

- Not a separate section — fats are integrated through cooking, dressing, or whole food sources
- Olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds, fatty fish

**Small Side = FERMENT (Probiotic Addition)**

- 2–4 tablespoons fermented vegetables, or
- Yogurt/kefir as part of the meal, or
- Miso-based dressing/soup

**Sprinkle/Drizzle = BOOSTER (Polyphenol Amplifier)**

- Herbs, spices, seeds, or other polyphenol-rich additions
- The finishing touch that multiplies benefits

This isn't a rigid prescription. It's a flexible template you can adapt to any meal.

## Component 1: Two Plants (Fiber Foundation)

The foundation of every EatoBiotics meal is two different plant-based components that provide fiber, nutrients, and prebiotic compounds.

<ChapterStat value="50%" label="of your plate" sublabel="should be plants — the fiber foundation of every meal" color="green" />

### Plant 1: Vegetables (Always Include)

Your first plant should always be vegetables — ideally a variety.

**Non-starchy vegetables (unlimited quantities):**

- **Leafy greens:** spinach, kale, arugula, mixed greens, romaine, chard
- **Cruciferous:** broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts, cabbage, bok choy
- **Colourful vegetables:** bell peppers, tomatoes, carrots, beets, eggplant, zucchini, squash
- **Alliums:** onions, garlic, leeks, scallions
- **Others:** asparagus, green beans, snap peas, mushrooms, cucumber, celery

**Starchy vegetables (moderate portions):**

- Sweet potatoes, regular potatoes (white, red, Yukon gold)
- Winter squash (butternut, acorn, kabocha)
- Corn, parsnips, turnips, rutabaga

**How to include:**

- **Raw:** Salads, slaws, crudités
- **Cooked:** Roasted, steamed, sautéed, grilled, stir-fried
- **Fermented:** Counts as both Plant 1 and Ferment (kimchi, sauerkraut)

**Target volume:** At least 1–2 cups cooked vegetables, or 2–3 cups raw greens per meal

**Variety matters:** Don't eat the same vegetables every day. Rotate colours, types, and preparations.

### Plant 2: Legumes, Whole Grains, or More Vegetables

Your second plant adds additional fiber, often with protein and resistant starch.

**Option A: Legumes (Ideal — provides both fiber and protein)**

- Lentils (red, green, black), chickpeas, black beans, pinto beans, kidney beans
- White beans (cannellini, great northern, navy), split peas, edamame

<ChapterCallout type="science">
**Why Legumes Are the Ideal Plant 2**

Legumes count as both Plant 2 and Protein — they're dual-purpose powerhouses. One cup of cooked legumes provides 12–16g fiber and 15–18g protein, plus rich resistant starch and prebiotic compounds. No other single food delivers this combination. When possible, make legumes your Plant 2 and your protein at the same meal.
</ChapterCallout>

**Option B: Whole Grains**

- Brown rice, black rice, wild rice, quinoa
- Farro, barley, oats, bulgur, millet, buckwheat
- Whole grain bread or pasta (occasionally)

Provide 3–6g fiber per cup cooked, complex carbohydrates for energy, resistant starch (especially if cooked and cooled), beta-glucans (oats, barley).

**Option C: Starchy Vegetables**

- Sweet potatoes, regular potatoes (cooked and cooled for resistant starch), winter squash

**Option D: Double Down on Vegetables**

- If you're eating lower-carb or just want more vegetables, make both Plant 1 and Plant 2 different vegetables
- Example: Salad greens + roasted Brussels sprouts

**Target volume:** ½ to 1 cup legumes or grains, or 1–2 cups additional vegetables

### Why Two Plants Matter

This is how you hit 30–40g fiber daily and 30+ different plants weekly:

- **Breakfast:** 2 plants (e.g., oats + berries, or vegetables + potato)
- **Lunch:** 2 plants (e.g., quinoa + mixed vegetables, or legumes + salad greens)
- **Dinner:** 2 plants (e.g., brown rice + roasted vegetables, or lentils + sautéed greens)

**Total: 6 different plant-based components daily = 40+ different plants weekly** when you rotate your choices.

Each plant brings unique fibers, resistant starches, polyphenols, and nutrients. Diversity is the goal.

## Component 2: Quality Protein

Protein is essential for tissue repair, satiety, blood sugar stability, and immune function.

### How Much Protein Per Meal

**Target: 20–40g protein per meal**

- Sedentary to moderately active: aim for 20–25g
- Active/training: aim for 30–40g
- Spread protein across 3–4 meals (don't load it all at dinner)

### Protein Sources (Choose One or Combine)

**Tier 1: Legumes (Best — provides protein + fiber)**

- 1 cup cooked legumes = 15–18g protein + 12–16g fiber
- Counts as both Plant 2 and Protein
- This is your ideal option when possible

**Tier 2: Fish and Seafood**

- Salmon, sardines, mackerel, herring (fatty fish — omega-3s)
- White fish (cod, halibut, tilapia), shellfish (shrimp, scallops, mussels)
- 3–4 oz serving = 20–25g protein

**Tier 3: Poultry**

- Chicken breast or thighs, turkey, duck
- 3–4 oz serving = 25–30g protein

**Tier 4: Eggs**

- Whole eggs — 2–3 eggs = 12–18g protein
- Often combined with other protein sources or legumes

**Tier 5: Tempeh and Tofu**

- Tempeh (fermented): 3 oz = 15g protein + probiotics
- Tofu (not fermented but still good): 3 oz = 10g protein

**Tier 6: Greek Yogurt or Kefir**

- 1 cup Greek yogurt = 15–20g protein + probiotics
- Counts as both Protein and Ferment — usually at breakfast

**Tier 7: Red Meat (Occasional)**

- Grass-fed beef, bison, lamb
- 3–4 oz serving = 20–25g protein
- Limit to 1–2 times weekly maximum

**Tier 8: Cheese (Small Amounts)**

- Provides protein but is calorie-dense
- Use as a flavour addition (1–2 oz) rather than main protein

<ChapterCallout type="note">
**Combining Proteins**

You don't have to choose just one protein source. Combinations often work better:

- **Eggs + legumes** (e.g., eggs with black beans)
- **Greek yogurt + nuts** (breakfast)
- **Small amount of fish + legumes** (e.g., sardines on top of white bean salad)
- **Tempeh + seeds** (complete amino acid profile)

Combining gives you more diverse nutrients and makes hitting your 20–40g target easier.
</ChapterCallout>

## Component 3: Healthy Fats (Integrated)

Fats aren't a separate section on your plate — they're integrated throughout the meal for satiety, nutrient absorption, and flavour.

### How Much Fat Per Meal

**Target: 15–25g fat per meal**

This happens automatically when you use quality fat sources:

- 2 tablespoons olive oil = 28g fat
- ½ avocado = 15g fat
- 1 oz nuts (small handful) = 14–18g fat
- Fatty fish = 10–15g fat per 4 oz serving

You don't need all of these — choose 1–2 fat sources per meal and you'll hit the target naturally.

**Additional fat sources:**

- **Tahini** — in dressings, drizzled on bowls, mixed into hummus (1–2 tablespoons)
- **Olives** — whole olives added to Mediterranean-style meals (5–10 olives)
- **Fatty fish** — when fish is your protein, it also provides your healthy fats
- **Coconut** — use sparingly in curries (high in saturated fat)

<ChapterCallout type="warning">
**Fats to Minimise**

- **Butter:** Use in small amounts if you enjoy it, but prioritise olive oil
- **Industrial seed oils:** Soybean, corn, canola — often highly processed
- **Trans fats:** Avoid completely (partially hydrogenated oils)

The goal isn't fat-free. It's choosing fats that actively support your microbiome and reduce inflammation, rather than fats that work against you.
</ChapterCallout>

## Component 4: Fermented Foods (Probiotic Addition)

Every meal should include a fermented component to continuously support microbiome diversity.

### How Much Fermented Food

**Target: 2–4 tablespoons fermented vegetables, or ½ cup yogurt/kefir per meal**

You need 2–3 fermented food servings daily total, so not every single meal requires them — but try to include at least one fermented food at 2–3 meals.

<ChapterStat value="2–3" label="servings daily" sublabel="of fermented foods to maintain microbiome diversity" color="teal" />

### Fermented Food Options

**At any meal:**

- **Sauerkraut or kimchi** — 2–4 tablespoons on the side, mixed into grain bowls or salads, on top of proteins
- **Miso** — mixed into dressing (miso-tahini, miso-ginger), miso soup as a side (1 tablespoon per serving)
- **Pickled vegetables** — naturally fermented pickles (not vinegar-pickled), fermented carrots, beets, or other vegetables

**At breakfast:**

- **Yogurt or kefir** — plain Greek yogurt (½ to 1 cup), kefir (½ to 1 cup). Counts as both Protein and Ferment

**At lunch or dinner:**

- **Tempeh** — fermented soy product. Counts as both Protein and Ferment (3–4 oz serving)
- **Kombucha** — 4–8 oz with the meal (probiotics + hydration)
- **Yogurt-based sauces** — tzatziki (Greek yogurt with cucumber, garlic, dill), raita (Indian yogurt with cucumber and spices)

## Component 5: The Booster (Polyphenol Amplifier)

This is the finishing touch — the herbs, spices, seeds, or other polyphenol-rich additions that multiply the meal's benefits.

### Booster Options

**Fresh herbs (generous amounts):**

- Parsley, cilantro, basil, mint, dill
- Chop and sprinkle liberally
- High in polyphenols, count toward plant diversity

**Spices (use abundantly):**

- Turmeric (add to grain dishes, eggs, soups)
- Cumin (pairs with legumes)
- Cinnamon (breakfast dishes, oatmeal)
- Paprika, oregano, thyme, rosemary
- Black pepper (enhances turmeric absorption)

**Seeds (1–2 tablespoons):**

- Ground flaxseed, chia seeds, hemp seeds, pumpkin seeds, sesame seeds

**Other boosters:**

- Lemon or lime juice (vitamin C, polyphenols)
- Apple cider vinegar (in dressings — provides acetic acid)
- Nutritional yeast (B vitamins, cheesy flavour)
- Dried seaweed (minerals, umami)
- Cacao nibs or dark chocolate shavings (breakfast or dessert)

<ChapterCallout type="food">
**The Booster Is Small but Mighty**

The booster is small in volume but mighty in impact — it adds flavour, polyphenols, and additional plant diversity without adding bulk or calories. Every fresh herb and spice you add counts toward your 30+ different plants weekly. A handful of cilantro, a squeeze of lemon, a sprinkle of cumin — these tiny additions compound into enormous benefits over time.
</ChapterCallout>

## The Formula Across Meal Types

### Breakfast EatoBiotics Plates

**Template:** Grain or Protein Base + Fruit or Vegetable + Yogurt/Kefir + Nuts/Seeds + Spices

**Savory example:**

- 2 scrambled eggs (Protein) + sautéed spinach and tomatoes (Plant 1) + black beans (Plant 2 + Protein) + avocado (Fat) + sauerkraut on the side (Ferment) + hot sauce and cilantro (Booster)

**Sweet example:**

- Oatmeal (Plant 2) + berries (Plant 1) + Greek yogurt (Protein + Ferment) + walnuts and ground flaxseed (Fat + Booster) + cinnamon (Booster)

**Smoothie example:**

- Frozen berries (Plant 1) + spinach (Plant 1) + oats or chia seeds (Plant 2) + Greek yogurt or kefir (Protein + Ferment) + almond butter or flaxseed (Fat) + cinnamon, ginger (Booster)

### Lunch EatoBiotics Plates

**Template:** Base (Grain or Greens) + Vegetables + Protein + Ferment + Dressing (Fat + Booster)

**Grain bowl:** Quinoa (Plant 2) + roasted vegetables (Plant 1) + chickpeas (Protein) + kimchi (Ferment) + tahini dressing with lemon (Fat + Booster) + pumpkin seeds (Booster)

**Salad:** Mixed greens, tomatoes, cucumbers, carrots (Plant 1) + lentils (Plant 2 + Protein) + feta cheese (extra Protein) + sauerkraut (Ferment) + olive oil dressing (Fat) + sunflower seeds, fresh herbs (Booster)

**Leftover transformation:** Last night's dinner over greens + add fresh vegetables + add a fermented component + new dressing or booster = completely different meal

### Dinner EatoBiotics Plates

**Template:** Protein + Two Vegetables + Grain/Starch + Ferment + Finishing Touches

**Simple roast:** Roasted chicken thighs (Protein) + roasted Brussels sprouts and sweet potatoes (Plant 1 + Plant 2) + olive oil drizzle (Fat) + sauerkraut on the side (Ferment) + fresh rosemary, lemon zest (Booster)

**Stir-fry:** Tempeh or shrimp (Protein + Ferment if tempeh) + tons of vegetables (Plant 1: broccoli, peppers, bok choy, mushrooms) + brown rice (Plant 2) + sesame oil (Fat) + kimchi on top (Ferment) + ginger, garlic, green onions (Booster)

**Curry:** Chickpeas (Plant 2 + Protein) + spinach, tomatoes, cauliflower (Plant 1) + coconut milk (Fat) + serve over quinoa or with whole grain naan + mango chutney with live cultures (Ferment) + cilantro, lime, garam masala (Booster)

### Omnivore

Most flexible — all options available. Typical: vegetables + legumes or whole grains + fish, poultry, eggs, or legumes + olive oil, avocado, nuts + any ferment + herbs, spices, seeds.

### Plant-Based / Vegan

Emphasise legumes and fermented soy. Legumes at lunch and dinner (double duty as Plant 2 and Protein). Ferments: sauerkraut, kimchi, miso, tempeh, coconut yogurt. Fats: olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds, tahini. Key: Make sure you're eating enough total food to meet calorie and protein needs.

### Pescatarian

No meat, but fish and dairy are fine. Fish provides both protein and omega-3 fats. Yogurt and kefir serve as both Protein and Ferment. Highly compatible with the EatoBiotics template.

### Lower-Carb

Reducing grains and starchy vegetables — still need fiber from non-starchy sources. Make both Plant 1 and Plant 2 different vegetables (large portions). Higher protein proportion. More fats (olive oil, avocado, nuts, fatty fish).

<ChapterCallout type="warning">
**Lower-Carb and Fiber: The Challenge**

Hitting 30–40g fiber without grains and starchy vegetables requires eating substantial volumes of non-starchy vegetables and including nuts, seeds, and avocados. It can be done, but requires attention to fiber sources. Don't sacrifice fiber for carb restriction — your microbiome needs that fuel.
</ChapterCallout>

### Paleo / Primal

No grains or legumes. Plant 2 = starchy vegetables (sweet potatoes, squash) or more non-starchy vegetables. Large vegetable volumes required. Traditional Paleo is often too low in fiber for optimal gut health. A modified "Primal" approach that includes fermented foods and abundant vegetables works better.

### Keto

Very low carb (under 50g carbs daily) makes it very difficult to hit 30–40g fiber. You can get 20–25g fiber with careful planning, but reaching 40g is nearly impossible without some carbs from legumes or grains.

**Better option for most people:** Moderate low-carb (100–150g carbs daily from vegetables, legumes, some whole grains) supports both metabolic health and gut health.

## Common Questions About The EatoBiotics Plate

### "Do I need to measure portions?"

No. The visual template is sufficient:

- Half your plate is plants (eyeball it)
- Quarter is protein (roughly the size of your palm)
- Fats are integrated (a drizzle of oil, a quarter avocado, a small handful of nuts)
- Fermented foods are small additions (a few forkfuls)

After a few weeks, this becomes intuitive. You won't think about it.

### "What if I can't get all five components at every meal?"

That's fine. The ideal is all five, but:

- **At minimum,** ensure you have 2 Plants + Protein + Fat at every meal
- **Add Ferment** at 2–3 meals daily (not necessarily every meal)
- **Add Booster** whenever possible (it's easy — just herbs and spices)

Perfection isn't required. Consistency across the day and week is what matters.

### "Can I use this template for snacks?"

Yes, for substantial snacks:

- Apple slices (Plant 1) + almond butter (Fat + protein) + Greek yogurt (Protein + Ferment)
- Carrot and cucumber sticks (Plant 1) + hummus (Plant 2 + Protein + Fat) + fermented pickles (Ferment)

For lighter snacks: Just grab fruit, nuts, or yogurt. Not every eating occasion needs the full template — reserve it for meals.

### "What about dessert?"

The EatoBiotics approach doesn't forbid dessert, but it's not part of the template.

If you eat dessert: keep portions reasonable, choose options with some nutritional value (dark chocolate, fruit-based desserts, yogurt-based desserts), and don't eat dessert daily — make it occasional.

Many people find that when they're eating the EatoBiotics Plate consistently, sugar cravings decrease dramatically.

### "How do I apply this when eating out?"

**Restaurants:**

- Scan the menu for protein options (fish, chicken, legume-based dishes)
- Request extra vegetables (often available as a side)
